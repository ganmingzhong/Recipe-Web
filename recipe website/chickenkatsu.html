Japanese Classic Snack

Chicken Katsu

Ingredient

Main Ingredient:

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - pounded to 1/2 inch thickness
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 1 cup oil for frying, or as needed
  • 2 ounces dried kombu (dried kelp)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons grated fresh ginger
  • 3 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 2 small carrots, peeled and sliced into thin strips
  • 2 small cucumbers, peeled and sliced into small strips
  • 2 teaspoons sesame seeds

Instructions

Frying crispy Japanese Fried Chicken:

  1. Apply all the seasonings, salt and paper on the chicken breasts
  2. Fully dredge the well-seasoned chicken breasts in flour
  3. Beat and whisk the eggs
  4. Coat each chicken breast in egg, then press into the panko bread crumbs
  5. Fry the chicken katsu until golden brown

Mixing ingredients for Kombu Seaweek Salad:

  1. Cover kombu with water in saucepan and bring to a boil
  2. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until kombu has softened, about 5 minutes
  3. Drain and cool until easily handled, 5 to 10 minutes
  4. Slice rehydrated kombu into thin strips
  5. Whisk honey, soy sauce, ginger, and rice vinegar together in a large bowl
  6. Add kombu, carrots, cucumbers, and sesame seeds; toss to combine
  7. Let sit in the refrigerator until flavors combine, 10 to 15 minutes


Nutrition Facts (per serving)

  1. Calories:297
  2. Fat:11g
  3. Carbs:22g
  4. Protein:31g
